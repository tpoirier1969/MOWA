Current public identity

MOWA already describes itself broadly.

The current public site describes MOWA as a nonprofit made up of outdoor communicators, including writers, photographers, editors, video, lecturers, public relations specialists, and radio/television journalists. It also says MOWA supports members engaged in all forms of communication while mentoring the next generation.

The question this survey answers

Are current activities matching that mission?

Results are designed to be tallied as importance-versus-performance gaps, then compared against the bylaws and mission. That keeps the discussion grounded in member expectations instead of taste, nostalgia, or board-room weather systems.

Why this format works

It asks members to define the job before judging the tools.

If people start by arguing about the website, membership rules, awards, or social media, everyone retreats into opinions. This survey first asks what MOWA should be doing, then asks how well MOWA is doing it.
