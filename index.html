Next steps

Public identity

MOWA already includes more than writers.

The public site describes MOWA as a nonprofit of outdoor communicators and lists writers, photographers, editors, video, lecturers, public relations specialists, and radio/television journalists among the people it serves.

Member experience

It asks what people expected.

The questionnaire includes a direct section on whether MOWA has done what people wanted it to do when they signed up, what has been useful, and what more it could provide.

Practical result

It produces numbers and comments.

Results can be summarized as expectation-versus-delivery gaps, plus open comments for the board to review without making the questionnaire feel like a loaded ambush.

Why this format works

It asks members to define the job before judging the tools.

Rather than starting with a debate over the website, awards, membership process, or social media, this first asks what MOWA should be doing. Then it asks how well MOWA is doing those things.
